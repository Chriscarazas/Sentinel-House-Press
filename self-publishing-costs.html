Pricing

What professional publishing support actually costs.

Understand the real costs of editing, book design, formatting, ISBNs, distribution, author websites, marketing, revisions, and project management.

By Christopher CarazasSentinel House PressUpdated July 2, 2026

Independent authors make better decisions when publishing terms, responsibilities, costs, and tradeoffs are explained plainly. This guide addresses the practical questions beneath the marketing language.

Why costs vary

A polished book may require assessment, developmental editing, copyediting, proofreading, cover design, interior design, ebook formatting, ISBNs, proof copies, distribution setup, website work, launch assets, and project management. Manuscript length and condition change the cost substantially.

Editorial costs

Editing is often the largest investment because it is labor intensive and directly tied to word count, complexity, source material, and revision needs.

Design and production costs

Cover design, interior layout, print files, and ebook files should be scoped separately enough that authors understand what is being purchased and how revisions work.

Publishing and platform costs

ISBN purchases, platform fees, proofs, shipping, website hosting, domain registration, email tools, and sales systems may create direct third-party expenses.

Marketing costs

Marketing can include launch strategy, media materials, advertising, events, email systems, content production, and ongoing outreach. No ethical provider should promise that a larger budget guarantees sales.

A better budgeting method

Begin with the book’s goals, audience, quality standard, commercial expectations, and available resources. Prioritize the work that protects the reader experience and the author’s long-term control. Ask for defined deliverables, revision limits, payment milestones, contractor costs, and exclusions.

Sentinel House Press pricing
