The Studio is designed for teams with strong programs, research, or expertise and a specific communication project they need to complete.

Nonprofits and NGOs

Donor reports, annual reports, impact stories, program briefs, websites, and board-facing materials.

Typical problem: multiple contributors produce accurate material without a coherent public narrative.

Foundations and funders

Learning reports, portfolio summaries, grantee stories, strategy briefs, and public-facing research.

Typical problem: complex evidence needs to serve boards, partners, grantees, and public audiences at once.

Research and evaluation teams

Evaluation reports, evidence summaries, briefs, case studies, and accessible adaptations of technical work.

Typical problem: methodological rigor survives, but the finding readers need is buried.

Consultancies and experts

Thought leadership, capability materials, proposals, websites, reports, and reusable content systems.

Typical problem: deep expertise exists, but client-facing communication is inconsistent or delayed.

Education and healthcare organizations

Program reports, community materials, case studies, websites, and stakeholder communication.

Typical problem: specialized language must remain accurate while becoming understandable.

Mission-driven companies

Impact reporting, ESG narratives, community-investment communication, partner materials, and public websites.

Typical problem: the communication must be credible, specific, and resistant to inflated claims.

A strong fit

The project has substance and a finish line.

  • You have source material or access to the people who hold it.
  • You know which audience must understand or act.
  • You can appoint one accountable approver.
  • You welcome structural and editorial challenge.
  • You can review facts within agreed windows.
  • You have a realistic budget and deadline.

A poor fit

The scope is infinite and everyone has veto power.

  • You need daily communications management.
  • You expect unlimited revisions for a fixed fee.
  • No one can make a final decision.
  • Every stakeholder may reopen approved work.
  • The deadline requires skipping discovery and review.
  • You need legal, regulatory, or scientific sign-off from the Studio.
