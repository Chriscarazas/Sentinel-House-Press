Start with the project

Tell us what exists.
And what is not working.

You do not need polished language. A useful first inquiry explains the source material, audience, problem, desired deliverable, deadline, and decision process.

Include these six things

  1. Project type: report, website, brief, content system, or other defined communication.
  2. Source material: what already exists, approximate length, and current condition.
  3. Audience: who must understand, decide, fund, approve, or act.
  4. Problem: what is currently unclear, ineffective, delayed, or unfinished.
  5. Outcome: the finished deliverable you need.
  6. Timing: desired deadline and who must review the work.

A useful inquiry

“We have a 55-page draft evaluation report written by five contributors. The findings are accurate, but the executive summary is weak and the document has no consistent structure or visual hierarchy. The donor needs a polished PDF and editable Word version by October 15. Our evaluation director will handle factual review, and our executive director will approve the final document.”

Protect sensitive information

Describe the project first. Send confidential files later.

Do not attach beneficiary information, donor data, proprietary research, or confidential reports in the first message. Secure transfer can be arranged after fit and scope are confirmed.
