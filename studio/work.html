Composite demonstrations

Three common communication problems.
Three concrete transformations.

These examples are demonstrations, not fictional client claims. They show the decisions, deliverables, and outcomes the Studio is built to produce.

Demonstration 01 / Report

The finding is on page 43.

Starting point: a 64-page evaluation report assembled by eleven contributors. The strongest result appears halfway through the findings chapter. The executive summary lists activities but does not identify the decision.

Editorial intervention: rebuild the report around three priority questions, lead with the 38% completion finding, connect the result to implementation conditions, and separate evidence from recommendation.

Deliverables: revised architecture, rewritten executive summary, edited narrative, chart hierarchy, designed report, editable file, final PDF.

Practical result: board and donor readers can understand the result, limitation, and recommended action without excavating the entire report.

KEY FINDING38%

higher completion among participants receiving ongoing coaching

Recommended action: extend coaching and measure completion by support intensity.

Demonstration 02 / Website

The organization has 42 pages and no clear sentence.

Starting point: navigation mirrors the internal org chart. Program names overlap. The homepage opens with history, not purpose. Three audiences encounter the same generic call to action.

Editorial intervention: define the primary audience, write a one-sentence positioning statement, reduce the sitemap, create audience paths, and move proof beside the claims it supports.

Deliverables: message hierarchy, revised sitemap, navigation, homepage copy, five core-page drafts, CTA system, implementation brief.

Practical result: visitors can determine what the organization does, whether it is relevant to them, and what to do next within the first screen.

HOMEPAGE TEST5 sec

to understand the purpose, audience, proof, and next action

Internal vocabulary moves deeper into the site. Visitor language leads.

Demonstration 03 / Content system

The research launched once and then disappeared.

Starting point: an 80-page research report, one webinar recording, and several interviews exist, but the team keeps writing new posts from scratch.

Editorial intervention: extract one central argument, map six audience needs, develop an anchor article, and adapt the material into a coordinated publication sequence.

Deliverables: executive brief, case study, website story, partner email, newsletter, six talking points, message bank.

Practical result: the original investment continues producing useful communication without turning the same paragraph into twelve mildly altered social posts.
