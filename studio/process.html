How the Studio works

A defined path from source material
to finished communication.

We approve the logic before polishing the language, and approve the content before decorating the page. Revolutionary, apparently.

01 / DIAGNOSE

Establish the purpose.

We review the source material, intended audience, required action, deadline, factual constraints, stakeholders, and approval structure.

Outputs

  • Project brief
  • Confirmed deliverables
  • Source inventory
  • Audience and decision statement
  • Review calendar
02 / STRUCTURE

Build the reading path.

Before extensive rewriting, we determine what belongs, what repeats, what is missing, and what the reader needs first.

Outputs

  • Report outline, sitemap, or content architecture
  • Message hierarchy
  • Priority findings or claims
  • Approved section sequence
03 / DEVELOP

Strengthen the communication.

We rewrite, edit, synthesize, translate technical language, connect evidence to meaning, and shape each section for its intended reader.

Outputs

  • Revised draft
  • Executive summary or homepage narrative
  • Headings and transitions
  • Fact-check queries
  • First formal review round
04 / DESIGN

Make the hierarchy visible.

For documents and websites, design follows the argument. Findings, decisions, evidence, and actions receive distinct visual roles.

Outputs

  • Page or screen design
  • Chart and callout treatment
  • Template or component system
  • Design review round
05 / FINISH

Proof, deliver, document.

We complete proofreading, final corrections, file preparation, and a documented handoff.

Outputs

  • Editable source files
  • Final PDF or implementation-ready copy
  • Asset folder
  • Handoff notes
  • Closeout confirmation

Approval discipline

One approver. Defined review windows. Limited rounds.

Subject-matter experts may review facts. One designated person consolidates decisions. Changes after approval are handled through a change order when they alter scope, schedule, or completed work.
