Investment orientation

Professional publishing is scoped, not guessed.

Sentinel House Press is not positioned as the cheapest upload service. Pricing reflects manuscript length, complexity, editorial stage, specialist costs, revision exposure, project management, and the judgment required to coordinate the whole book.

01

Assessment

Paid, fixed-fee work with a defined review depth and a written recommendation.

02

Editorial

Fixed project fee, staged by editorial level, manuscript length, and review rounds.

03

Production

Fixed package plus clearly itemized contractor, platform, and third-party costs.

04

Platform

Fixed project fee or capped retainer with explicit hours, access, and deliverables.

Capacity: no more than two major publishing engagements at one time. Start dates are reserved through deposit and written scope.

Pricing discipline

Price the whole project, not just the visible labor.

Authors are not buying keystrokes. They are buying judgment, orchestration, coordination, risk reduction, and a finished result.

Pricing accounts for labor, contractor costs, software, communication, revisions, project management, payment processing, risk, overhead, founder compensation, and profit.

  • Non-refundable deposit to reserve capacity
  • Milestone payments tied to defined stages
  • Written change orders for work outside scope
  • Defined revision rounds
  • Rush fees when compressed timelines displace other work
